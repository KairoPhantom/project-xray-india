# Evidence and publication policy

## Source hierarchy
1. Primary official record: signed contract, order, filing, audit report, court order.
2. Official public statement/dashboard.
3. Independent technical report or named expert assessment.
4. Reputable reporting with transparent attribution.
5. Public/social submission—unverified until corroborated.

Higher rank is not automatically truthful; it determines description and verification needs.

## Language rules
- Say “not located in the searched sources,” not “does not exist.”
- Say “officials stated,” not “the cause was,” unless independently established.
- Say “risk indicator” or “inconsistency,” not “corruption probability.”
- State what evidence does not establish.

## Publication gate
Two-person review for real cases. One reviewer must be independent of extraction. Engineering claims require qualified review or must remain clearly attributed.

## Corrections
Preserve original version, publish correction reason/time, notify affected subscribers where material, and never erase the audit trail except legally required private-data redaction with a tombstone.
