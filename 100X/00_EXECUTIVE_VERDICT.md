# Executive verdict — brutal version

## Verdict
**Worth building: yes, conditionally.**  
**High potential public value: yes.**  
**Automatically high traction: no.**  
**Venture-scale software from the current concept alone: unlikely.**  
**A respected open-source civic infrastructure project with institutional adoption: plausible.**

## Scorecard
| Dimension | Score | Judgment |
|---|---:|---|
| Problem severity | 9/10 | Infrastructure evidence is fragmented, consequential and difficult to verify. |
| User pain | 8/10 | Journalists, civic groups, auditors and engineers repeatedly reconstruct the same provenance chain. |
| Technical feasibility | 8/10 | Core components exist; integration and evidence governance are the hard parts. |
| Differentiation today | 5/10 | Current reference app is a thin CRUD dossier. The wedge must be sharper. |
| Defensibility | 6/10 | Code is copyable; a trusted evidence protocol, connector corpus, benchmarks and community are harder to copy. |
| Organic mass-market traction | 3/10 | Civic evidence is not a daily consumer habit. |
| Institutional traction | 8/10 | Strong if pilots save professionals time and produce defensible outputs. |
| Legal/reputational risk | 9/10 | Unsupported publication, PII exposure and defamatory framing can end the project. |
| Sustainable OSS business | 6/10 | Managed hosting, assurance, connectors, training and support can work; donations alone are fragile. |

## The make-or-break insight
The valuable product is not “AI finds corruption.” That framing is unsafe, crowded and impossible to validate. The valuable product is:

> **A reproducible evidence workflow for Indian public infrastructure: every statement linked to an original record, exact passage, retrieval event, review decision, contradiction and correction history.**

## Reasons to build
- The input data is fragmented across portals, PDFs, notices, audits, court records, RTI replies and field observations.
- Existing investigative systems are broad; an infrastructure-specific evidence lifecycle can be much easier to use.
- OCDS/OC4IDS provide standards to align with instead of inventing a closed model.
- Modern document parsing and frontier models reduce extraction cost, while a human gate preserves trust.
- Open source improves auditability and lowers adoption friction for newsrooms and civil-society organizations.

## Reasons it could fail
1. Building a technically impressive platform before securing three committed design partners.
2. Treating incomplete portal data as evidence of wrongdoing.
3. Publishing AI-generated claims without independent review.
4. Trying to crawl all of India before one local workflow works.
5. Confusing GitHub stars with usage and impact.
6. Underestimating source licensing, portal terms, takedowns, PII and defamation risk.
7. Building Aleph, DocumentCloud, OCR and a data warehouse from scratch.
8. No permanent owner for corrections, incidents, backups and factual disputes.

## Go/no-go test
Proceed only if, within 30 days, at least **three design partners** agree to test the workflow and provide real feedback, and at least **one partner completes a dossier 30% faster** without lowering citation quality. If not, narrow the product or stop.
