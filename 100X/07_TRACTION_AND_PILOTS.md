# Traction, pilots and impact

## Traction truth
This is unlikely to become a mass-consumer viral product. The credible path is high-value professional adoption, public-interest outcomes and a contributor ecosystem.

## Design partners
Recruit exactly five: investigative newsroom, civic/RTI group, civil engineer or infrastructure monitor, academic/audit lab and one authority/open-government team willing to respond.

## 30-day validation
- 20 problem interviews;
- 3 signed pilot commitments;
- 2 historical dossier replay tests;
- 1 live controlled dossier;
- benchmark current workflow time and citation error rate;
- publish what failed.

## Pilot success gates
- at least 30% reduction in median dossier preparation time;
- at least 95% of published claims open to the exact cited record/span;
- zero unsupported public claims;
- reviewers prefer the workflow over spreadsheets/doc folders;
- partner repeats the workflow without founder supervision;
- one external correction or official response is handled transparently.

## Distribution loops
1. Every public dossier includes a reproducible evidence capsule and “fork this methodology.”
2. Connector breakage reports invite domain contributors.
3. Monthly evidence clinics convert users into maintainers.
4. Standards-compatible exports make the project useful to existing ecosystems.
5. Publish benchmark datasets and parser scorecards, not promotional AI demos.
6. Case partners co-author methodology notes and training material.

## Avoid vanity metrics
GitHub stars, crawled pages and generated claims are secondary. Track active research teams, completed reviewed dossiers, citation reopen rate, corrections, authority responses, records later disclosed and time saved.

## Impact boundary
Do not claim the platform caused accountability outcomes without contribution evidence. Use the ladder: dossier used -> record requested -> response received -> record corrected/disclosed -> formal review -> repair/recovery/policy change.
