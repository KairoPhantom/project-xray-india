# Open-source governance and sustainable business

## Licence recommendation
Apache-2.0 is reasonable for the core. Conduct a dependency and data-licence review before release. Keep evidence-policy rules, schemas, connector SDK and public dossier renderer open.

## Governance
- public roadmap and decision records;
- maintainer roles and conflict-of-interest policy;
- security disclosure and factual correction channels;
- connector ownership and deprecation policy;
- reproducible releases and signed artifacts;
- community council only after real contributors exist;
- no pay-to-suppress, pay-to-rank or secret integrity scoring.

## Sustainable revenue
1. Managed private hosting for newsrooms/CSOs.
2. Support, training and workflow design.
3. Custom lawful connectors and data conversion.
4. Assurance packages: deployment, backup, security and evidence-process audits.
5. Institutional grants for open standards, language support and benchmark corpora.
6. Optional enterprise collaboration features, while keeping integrity logic open.

## What not to monetize
- accusation scores;
- sale of sensitive submissions;
- preferential removal of public evidence;
- hidden source ranking;
- raw personal data brokerage.

## Economic reality
Donations and stars rarely fund 24/7 incident response. Production operation needs a named legal/operator entity, budget, insurance/counsel as appropriate, moderation capacity and sustainable maintenance. Separate the open-source project from any editorial publisher so responsibility is explicit.
